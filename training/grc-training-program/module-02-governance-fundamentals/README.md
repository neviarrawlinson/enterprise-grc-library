# Module 2: Governance Fundamentals

## Objective
Understand the role of governance in a GRC framework, how it aligns with business objectives, and how to create the foundational documents that define oversight and accountability.

## What You'll Learn
- What IT Governance is and why it matters
- Governance vs. Management
- Key governance documents: charters, policies, frameworks
- Introduction to COBIT, ISO/IEC 38500, and ITIL
- Real-world examples of governance structures

---

## Section 1: What is IT Governance?

- Definition of governance in an IT and GRC context
- Governance vs. management (who decides vs. who executes)
- The role of governance in enforcing compliance, ensuring accountability, and aligning tech with business strategy

### Reading:
- [ISACA: What is IT Governance?](https://www.isaca.org/resources/news-and-trends/isaca-now-blog/2021/what-is-it-governance)

## Section 2: Core Components of a Governance Program

- Governance Committee or Council
- Governance Framework (e.g., COBIT, ISO 38500)
- Policies, Standards, and Charters
- Metrics and accountability reporting (e.g., KPIs, dashboards)

### Templates to Study:
- Governance Committee Charter
- Policy Governance Framework
- IT Governance Structure Diagram

---

## Section 3: Governance Frameworks

| Framework | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| COBIT 2019 | A governance framework for enterprise IT, aligned with business objectives |
| ISO/IEC 38500 | Corporate governance of information technology, emphasizing principles and evaluation |
| ITIL | Service management framework, often used in governance of service delivery and operations |

## Summary

Governance provides the structure that ensures IT supports and extends organizational strategies. Understanding how to build charters, enforce policies, and report on performance is foundational to any GRC role.
