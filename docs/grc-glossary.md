# GRC Glossary of Terms

A curated glossary of common terms, acronyms, and frameworks used in Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) programs. This reference is ideal for onboarding, documentation, training, and stakeholder education.

---

## A

- **Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):** A documented policy outlining rules for how company systems, data, and equipment can be used by employees or contractors.

- **Audit:** A formal review of systems, processes, or documents to ensure compliance with internal policies and external standards (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001).

---

## B

- **Business Continuity Plan (BCP):** A documented plan outlining how business operations will continue during and after a disruption or disaster.

- **Business Impact Analysis (BIA):** An assessment that evaluates how disruptions affect critical business functions and the resources required for recovery.

---

## C

- **Compliance:** Adherence to laws, regulations, standards, and internal policies.

- **Control:** A process, policy, or mechanism implemented to mitigate risk and ensure compliance.

---

## D

- **Data Classification:** The process of organizing data based on sensitivity or impact (e.g., Public, Internal, Confidential).

- **Due Diligence:** Investigation or research conducted prior to engaging with third parties or implementing new systems.

---

## G

- **Governance:** The framework of rules, roles, processes, and policies used to guide and oversee business and IT decision-making.

---

## I

- **Incident Response Plan (IRP):** A documented process for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security incidents.

- **Internal Controls:** Processes implemented by an organization to ensure integrity, security, and compliance.

---

## K

- **Key Risk Indicator (KRI):** A measurable value that signals increasing risk exposure in a specific area.

- **Key Performance Indicator (KPI):** A metric used to track performance and progress toward business or compliance goals.

---

## N

- **NIST (National Institute of Standards and Technology):** U.S. agency that develops cybersecurity and risk management frameworks like NIST CSF and NIST 800-53.

---

## P

- **PBC (Provided By Client):** Documentation or evidence requested by auditors to assess compliance.

- **Policy:** A formally documented principle or rule adopted by an organization to guide decisions and actions.

---

## R

- **Risk Assessment:** The process of identifying, analyzing, and prioritizing risks to organizational assets.

- **RCA (Root Cause Analysis):** A method used to identify the underlying cause(s) of incidents or problems.

---

## S

- **SOC 2 (System and Organization Controls):** A standard for managing customer data based on five trust principles: security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy.

- **Standard Operating Procedure (SOP):** A set of step-by-step instructions for recurring tasks and procedures.

---

## V

- **Vendor Risk Management (VRM):** The process of assessing, monitoring, and mitigating risks associated with third-party vendors.
